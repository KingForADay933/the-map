Continued Learning

School ending doesn't mean learning stops. The people who keep growing after graduation are the ones who stay curious and know where to look.

2 min read
TL;DR
  • Free, high-quality education is more accessible now than at any point in history. Crash Course, freeCodeCamp, and YouTube cover almost everything.
  • Learning toward a concrete goal, a skill, a career change, a project, is far more sustainable than learning abstractly.
  • Even 15 minutes of intentional daily learning compounds into significant knowledge over a year.
  • Teach what you're learning to someone else. Explaining a concept is one of the best tests of whether you actually understand it.

Learning Has Never Been More Accessible

Anyone with an internet connection has access to more educational content than any university library in history. The barrier isn't access anymore. It's knowing where to look and building the habit of actually using it.

Free courses and tutorials exist for nearly everything: accounting, video game development, coding, computer science, graphic design, copywriting, Adobe software, audio mixing, music theory, music production, video editing, and far more. If you want to learn it, the resources are out there. It's a matter of finding the right content and putting in consistent effort.

Free Resources Worth Knowing

Crash Course (YouTube)
youtube.com/@crashcourse. Hundreds of free video series covering history, science, economics, psychology, literature, philosophy, and more. High production value and genuinely engaging.
Study Hall (YouTube)
youtube.com/@studyhall. A collaboration between Crash Course and Arizona State University focused on college-level subjects and study skills.
Khan Academy
khanacademy.org. Free, structured courses covering math, science, computing, and test prep (SAT, LSAT, MCAT). Particularly strong for building foundational skills that formal education may have left gaps in.
freeCodeCamp
freecodecamp.org. Free coding curriculum covering web development, Python, data science, and more. Structured, project-based, and completely free.
YouTube (general)
Search for "[subject] + full course" or "[subject] + tutorial" on YouTube. Channels exist for almost every topic imaginable, from beginner to expert level. Quality varies, so read the comments and look for channels with consistent, well-reviewed content.

Paid Resources Worth the Investment

Study.com
study.com. Paid subscription (verify current pricing). Structured courses across dozens of subjects, including options to earn college credit. Useful for people who prefer guided learning with assessments.
Sophia Learning
sophia.org. Paid subscription (verify current pricing). Self-paced courses that transfer as college credit at many institutions, a cost-effective way to earn credit outside of a traditional classroom.

Building the Habit

Seeking out knowledge is a habit. The more consistently you do it, the easier it gets. A few things that help:

  • Start small. Even 15 minutes of intentional learning a day compounds into significant knowledge over a year.
  • Connect it to something you want. Learning toward a concrete goal, a new skill, a career change, a project, is far more sustainable than learning abstractly.
  • Use dead time. Commutes, chores, and workouts are opportunities to listen to podcasts, audiobooks, or educational content. How much dead time you have varies. The point is to find yours, not to manufacture it.
  • Take notes. Writing down what you learn dramatically improves retention. Even a brief summary after a video makes a difference.
  • Teach someone. Explaining something to another person is one of the most effective ways to truly understand it yourself.
A useful mindset

The people who stay curious tend to stay interesting, to employers, to others, and to themselves. Continued learning isn't just about career advancement. It's about having a more capable life.
