Pathways

Not sure where to start, or want to work through a specific situation from beginning to end? These curated sequences pull together the most relevant pages in the right order.

Pathway 01

Starting College or a Trade

For anyone about to begin a degree, certification, or apprenticeship. Covers everything from picking a direction and understanding the money to building the habits that make you actually succeed once you're in it.

01
Choosing a Major or Trade

Research your options before committing. What the different paths cost, what they lead to, and why trades deserve a serious look.
02
Financial Literacy Overview

Before you take on any debt, understand how money works. Income, expenses, emergency funds, and the order of operations.
03
Financial Aid & the FAFSA

How to file the FAFSA, understand your award letter, and make smart decisions about grants, work-study, and loans. File before you commit to a school.
04
The College Journey

Choosing the right school, surviving academically and financially, using the free resources most students ignore, and preparing for what comes after.
05
Effective Learning Techniques

Active recall, spaced repetition, and how to build a study system before your first semester, not after you fail your first exam.
06
Note-Taking & Knowledge Management

How to move from recording information to actually retaining it. The PARA system and tools that scale from a single class to a career.
07
Time Management & Productivity

Prioritization frameworks, how to avoid burnout, and why procrastination is usually anxiety in disguise.
08
The Working Student's Guide

If you're balancing a job and school, this covers gap-based study strategy, energy management, and how to communicate with employers and professors.
09
Debt Management & Building Credit

Federal vs. private loans, repayment options, and how your credit score works. Know this before the first payment is due.
Checkpoint Before taking on student loans: have you exhausted grants and scholarships, compared actual net price between schools (not sticker price), and calculated what monthly loan payments will look like on an entry-level salary in your target field?
Pathway 02

Landing Your First Job

From no resume to your first day, and making sure you're not leaving money on the table when the offer comes in.

01
Career Research

Before you apply anywhere, know what the role actually involves, what the market pays, and whether the field is growing or shrinking.
02
Building a Strong Resume

How to build one from scratch with limited experience and get past the ATS filters most employers use before a human ever sees your application.
03
Networking & Professional Relationships

Most opportunities come through people, not job boards. Building your network before you need it is the whole point.
04
Job Hunting

Where to look, how to follow up without being annoying, and the strategy that consistently outperforms mass-applying.
05
Interview Skills

Managing nerves, the STAR method for behavioral questions, and what to say when they ask if you have questions.
06
Salary Negotiation & Job Offers

Most people accept the first number. That's almost always a mistake. How to research your market rate and counter confidently.
07
Workplace Basics

What professionalism actually means, how to communicate with your manager, and what the first 90 days establish about how people see you.
08
Filing Taxes

Your first paycheck will have deductions you didn't expect. Your first April, you'll need to file. Here's what that actually involves.

Financial Literacy Overview

The core equation, the emergency fund, and the order of operations. Start here before anything else.
02
Budgeting

Five methods, which one fits how your brain works, and how to automate it so it actually sticks rather than getting abandoned in week two.
03
Filing Taxes

A real recurring financial obligation that catches most first-timers off guard. It's simpler than you expect. Here's exactly how to do it.
04
Debt Management & Building Credit

Good debt vs. bad debt, how your credit score is calculated, and how to start building it from scratch.

Credit Cards 101

The one rule that makes credit cards a tool rather than a trap, and how to choose your first card without getting burned.

Health Insurance 101

Premiums, deductibles, out-of-pocket maximums, and how to pick a plan. Healthcare costs are a real budget line item most people underestimate.

Moving Out

Moving into your first place involves more than a security deposit and a U-Haul. This path covers everything from finding the right place to protecting yourself legally and actually living like an adult once you're in it.

01
Budgeting

Know your numbers before you commit to a rent payment. The 30% rule, how to calculate total housing cost, and what's non-negotiable.
02
Finding a Place to Live

What to look for, what red flags to walk away from, and the questions to ask before you sign anything.
03
Basic Legal Literacy

What makes a contract binding, what to check before signing a lease, and what landlords can and cannot require. Read this before you sign.
04
Setting Up Your First Place

Utilities, mail forwarding, roommate agreements, and the first-week checklist most people figure out by stumbling through.
05
Renter's Insurance

$15 to $30 a month. Your landlord's policy covers the building, not your stuff. Get this before anything goes wrong.
06
Tenant Rights & Landlord Issues

Your rights as a renter, how to document issues, what landlords cannot do, and how to handle disputes including security deposit disagreements.
07
Cooking 101

Four techniques, a grocery budget approach, and five recipes that work. Cooking at home saves more money per month than almost any other single habit.
08
Laundry & Clothing Care

What the symbols mean, how to sort, cold vs. hot water, and how to handle stains before they set permanently.
Checkpoint Before you sign a lease: do you know your monthly take-home income, what utilities will cost, whether the landlord has a documented policy on security deposit returns, and have you photographed all existing damage?
Pathway 05

Healthcare Literacy

Coming off a parent's plan, getting coverage through a new job, or just trying to understand what you're actually paying for. Health is more than insurance. It's how you take care of yourself before you need the system.

01
Health Insurance 101

Premiums, deductibles, copays, HMO vs. PPO: every term decoded, and how to compare plans without just picking the cheapest monthly cost.
02
Budgeting

Healthcare costs are a real budget line item. Know your premium, estimated out-of-pocket, and how an emergency would hit your finances before picking a plan.
03
Nutrition Basics

Understanding what you're putting in your body and how it affects how you feel, think, and function. The choices that reduce how often you need the system.
04
Managing Emotions, Anxiety & Stress

Mental health is health. Evidence-based coping techniques, when to seek professional support, and what to do when it becomes more than you can manage alone.
05
Digital Safety

Your health insurance account, medical records, and pharmacy login are high-value targets. Protecting them takes the same habits as protecting your bank account.
06
Financial Literacy Overview

Medical bills are the most common cause of unexpected debt. An emergency fund is your first line of defense before you ever need to negotiate a bill.
Checkpoint Do you know your plan's deductible, out-of-pocket maximum, and whether your current doctor is in-network? Do you have an emergency fund large enough to cover your deductible? If not, those are worth addressing today, before you need a doctor.
